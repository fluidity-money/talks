Pros Cons
  • Don't need to adjust existing smart contracts
  • Anyone can retrieve their funds by submitting their transaction to the contract in the event of an emergency
  • Mainchain is aware of the contract state of what's in the rollup
  • Every interaction with the contract happens within the sequencer and as a second order effect causes atomicity and an updated state with any dependencies on the interaction
  • Compress the execution of the contract, not the output generated by a user interacting with the application's state machine
  • Lockup period or expensive verification
  • Less privacy than a state channel
  • Depends on sequencer liveliness
